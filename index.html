About the Global Population

The global population is projected to increase to about 9.7 billion people in 2050, compared to our current 7.7 billion. That’s an increase of 2 billion people in only thirty years! The result is even more people needing resources and water that are already rapidly depleting. Additionally, it will result in greater carbon emissions because of the products made for the increased population and the demand for space and resources. This could potentially mean an expansion into green area, resulting in deforestation and loss of rainforests, both of which are primary producers of oxygen.

About Freshwater

With water levels projected to rise 1.5 feet by the year 2050, many coastal cities are at risk. Cities such as Miami, New York City, and Calcutta could be some of the most impacted. Miami and the surrounding beaches already receive serious flooding because of rising sea levels, because they are flat areas with porous limestone underneath that allows for water to seep through. Additionally, most cities on the northeast coastline are projected to receive at least 30 days of serious flooding each year.

About Lost Cities

According to the UN, the demand for water will increase 55% by the year 2050 due to increased population growth. Economic growth will spark demand for greater productivity and increased water use to achieve that. Climate change is also going to disrupt our water supplies, and water will be needed to fulfill agricultural needs for the increased population. With these predictions, the UNEP projects that the number of people living in river basins under significant water stress will double by 2050, reaching 3.9 billion people.

Waste


X

About Waste

Annual waste generation is expected to jump to 3.4 billion tons annually by 2050, compared to 2.01 billion tons in 2016. With greater demand, production of plastics has increased 20-fold in the last 50 years. Though recycling has been implemented to help this problem, there are not enough efforts to recycle that will lead to significant impact. It is predicted that plastics in the ocean will outweigh fish, which, if not handled properly, will contaminate waterways and ecosystems for thousands of years.

Global Warming


X

About Global Warming

At our current level of carbon dioxide emissions, the global temperature will rise almost 2° by 2050. Immediate problems include melting snow caps and ice, directly causing rising sea levels. Additionally, it leads to more significantly warmer days, and most aspects of climate change will persist for many centuries, even if emissions are stopped. All in all, the IPCC has generated concrete data that climate change is real, and human activity is the main cause.

About Air Pollution

The number of deaths due to air pollution is projected to rise by 2050, killing more than 6 million people per year. The pollution, consisting of tiny particles in smoke and haze, ground-level ozone emitted by cars, and toxic components found in building materials, is leading to hazier skies and worsening respiratory diseases. It is predicted that 130 of every 1 million people will die prematurely in India, where air pollution is especially bad.

The Project

One of our members, Caroline, created a '2050 project' through her school. Her school team consists of Rachel Patton, Melina Sharp, Isabelle Skid, and herself. They were asked to find a solution to one of the world problems, come 2050. The team got chosen to go to a conference in Canada, and pitch their project in front of possible investors or collaborators. This is a quick overview of the team's project and how it will come together.

















After 1 Day, We Will Make...

4,485 kg AlCl3

3,880 kg CCl4

1 ton of Water

In the fall of 2018, their school decided to take a hands-off approach and allow the students to decide what problems they want to solve. There were no limits to what they could do. At the very first meeting, Caroline asked her team, "why don't we just make water?" Little did they know, that is a very hard thing to accomplish.

Carbon Dioxide (CO2) is the number one pollutant that is causing climate chance. Our process is to take pollution and combine it with unused recycling in order to form water.

Carbon Dioxide

By using a carbon capture machine, the carbon dioxide will be filtered out from fossil fuel emissions. We plan to collaborate with factories, starting with Georgia Power. Factories are required to have a carbon limit, therefore when they work with our project, less CO2 will be emitted and they can manufacture even more products.

Resources

As for resources, the project will use aluminum cans that are being recycled, yet not used. We will work with fire departments and other soda companies, in order to obtain these cans. The project will also require aluminum chloride and bleach in bulk. These will be combined with the aluminum cans in order to produce both hydorgen and oxygen gas.

Water

The hydrogen and oxygen gas will be combined together using fire, to produce the liquid water. Once the water is made, it will either be sent to Generosity or we will collaborate with a biodegradable water bottle company.
